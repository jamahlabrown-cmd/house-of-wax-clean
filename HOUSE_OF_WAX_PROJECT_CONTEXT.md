# House Of Wax Project Context

House Of Wax is a founder-led marketplace and culture platform.

The goal is to build a trusted platform for:

- Vinyl records
- CDs
- Cassettes
- Music memorabilia
- Posters
- Clothing
- Merch
- Dolls
- Accessories
- Culture goods
- Seller storefronts
- Knowledge and education

House Of Wax has three core areas:

## Marketplace

The marketplace is where items are listed, searched, and discovered.

This includes:

- Independent seller inventory
- House Of Wax Official inventory
- Branded merch
- Records
- CDs
- Cassettes
- Posters
- Clothing
- Memorabilia
- Collectibles
- Culture goods

## Knowledge Hub

The Knowledge Hub is where users learn, discover, and understand the culture.

This includes:

- Music education
- Record collecting education
- Format guides
- Genre and era education
- Trust and safety education
- Marketplace guidance
- House Of Wax editorial content

The Knowledge Hub is not mainly for promoting individual sellers. It is House Of Wax-owned education and culture content.

## My House Of Wax

My House Of Wax is the account and tools area.

This includes:

- Buyer account
- Seller tools
- Content admin
- Admin tools
- Test setup
- Release database tools
- Barcode/search tools

## Current product priority

Do not jump to payments, shipping, or full checkout yet.

The current foundation priority is:

1. Seller upload flow
2. Barcode/search/release lookup
3. Correct product data
4. Recommended best match
5. House Of Wax internal release database
6. Seller confidence and correction tools

Once product data and seller upload are strong, payments, shipping, and production architecture can come later.

## Important design principle

The app should feel like House Of Wax is guiding the user, not just showing raw database tools.

Search should:

- Be simple
- Feel smart
- Present one recommended best result
- Explain why it picked that result
- Let the seller correct it
- Learn from correction over time

## Team concept

This is a team project.

The founder leads the vision. The coding assistant protects the build, checks for mistakes, explains risks, and helps improve the product.

The assistant should not blindly agree with every idea. If something may break the app, confuse users, cost too much, or pull the project away from the mission, the assistant should say so respectfully and suggest a better path.
